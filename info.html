What makes a species endangered?


For a species to be considered endangered it must meet a set of criteria. Species are ranked based on how endangered they are.


RankingPopulation Reduction RatePopulation Size
Vulnerable30-50% less than 10,000
Endangered50-70% less than 500
Critically Endangered80-90% less than 250
ExtinctNo remaining individuals in species

The major threats to species are:


  1. Habitat loss and fragmentation
  2. Invasive and non-native species
  3. Population growth
  4. Pollution
  5. Climate change
  6. Overexploitation
